Main Causes of Poverty

Listed below are some of the main causes of poverty.

Inequality is a main cause of Poverty. Inequality fuels poverty by limiting opportunities for people based on factors like gender or ethnicity. For example, women often have fewer rights, making it harder for them to improve their situation. When combined with other risks, like being a widow or a refugee, these inequalities worsen, creating a cycle of poverty that's hard to escape.

Conflict is one of the biggest factors that can push people into poverty. War and crises can completely disrupt economies. For example, before the Syrian conflict started in 2011, only 10% of Syrians lived below the poverty line, but by the end of 2023, over 90% were living in poverty. While many conflicts today are more localized and get less international attention, large-scale crises like the one in Ukraine show how a major conflict can affect economies around the world.

This cycle is especially damaging for children, as malnourished mothers often have malnourished kids. The effects can last a lifetime, with stunted children earning less as adults and contributing to economic losses in countries like Ethiopia.

Extreme poverty and poor health are closely connected, with treatable illnesses becoming deadly in countries with weak healthcare. For women, pregnancy can be life-threatening, especially in patriarchal societies where they need male permission for care. Teenage mothers, particularly unmarried ones, face even more discrimination and inequality.
