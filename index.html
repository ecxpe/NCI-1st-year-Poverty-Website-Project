No Poverty: A Sustainable Development Goal

The first of the United Nations' 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) is to end poverty in all its forms everywhere. This ambitious goal aims to ensure that all people, especially the most vulnerable, have equal rights to economic resources, access to basic services, and ownership and control over land and other forms of property.

Eradicating poverty is not a task of charity, it is an act of justice and the key to unlocking an enormous human potential. Despite significant progress, more than 700 million people, or 10% of the world population, still live in extreme poverty, struggling to fulfill the most basic needs like health, education, and access to water and sanitation.

To achieve this goal, we need to create sound policy frameworks at the national, regional, and international levels, based on pro-poor and gender-sensitive development strategies, to support accelerated investment in poverty eradication actions.
