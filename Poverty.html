The current crisis in Chad

Poverty has profoundly impacted Chad, one of the poorest countries in the world, with far-reaching consequences for its people and development. Over two-thirds of the population lives below the poverty line, relying on subsistence agriculture and pastoralism in a country plagued by recurring droughts and limited arable land. This economic hardship has stifled access to basic necessities like education, healthcare, and clean water, leaving millions vulnerable to malnutrition and preventable diseases. The lack of infrastructure and job opportunities perpetuates cycles of poverty, while political instability and conflict further exacerbate the situation. Despite being rich in natural resources, such as oil, the wealth has not translated into significant poverty alleviation for the majority, highlighting deep inequalities. International aid and sustainable development initiatives remain crucial in addressing these challenges and fostering long-term stability in Chad.
